Your Role

BDR Overview

What a BDR does at Checkr and how you fit into the revenue engine.

As a BDR at Checkr, your job is to generate qualified pipeline for the AE team through strategic outbound prospecting. You are not responsible for closing deals — you are responsible for finding the right people, creating interest, and handing off qualified opportunities so AEs can advance them to close.

You sit at the very top of the funnel. The quality of your outreach — how well you research accounts, how targeted your messaging is, and how accurately you qualify — directly determines the quality of pipeline the team has to work with. Precision matters more than volume alone.

The Inbound Trap
Inbound is not where you will hit your number
Inbound feels productive because the prospect already has interest — but it is the result of previous outbound work or luck. Work inbounds appropriately, but deliberately block dedicated time every day for outbound. That is where your quota lives.
Where to Focus

Account Prioritization

It is your responsibility to identify the top priority accounts within your book each month. Pick a theme and go deep.

Each week or month, pick a theme — a particular industry or ICP segment — so you can use the same value prop, pain points, and mindset across your outreach. For example, spend a month focused on restaurants, get deep into that perspective, then switch to retail clothing stores. Become a mini-SME on each segment before moving on.

Working with Your AE

AE / BDR Partnership

The partnership is built on tight alignment and consistent communication. You function as a single pipeline-generation team.

Each Enterprise AE is partnered with a BDR (2 AEs to 1 BDR). Commercial BDRs support a broader book. Regardless of segment, the most effective partnerships share strategy, align on messaging, and review progress together every week.

What the BDR brings to the 1:1
  • Current pipeline — including accounts you're working outside of AE-provided lists (UserGems, events, ICP blitz)
  • Update on progress and current state of last week's AE-assigned accounts
  • Meetings happening this week — talk through handoffs so AEs are prepared for the call
  • Feedback loop: what SQLs happened, were they qualified? What should we start / stop / continue?
What the AE brings to the 1:1
  • Accounts with highest intent for BDR to focus on this week — based on DemandBase signals and any other focus areas for the month
  • Outreach plan for each assigned account — clear assignment of activity and messaging
  • Feedback on recent meetings: were the SQLs qualified? What additional context would help future outreach?
  • Strategic guidance on which verticals, personas, or signals to prioritize
📅
1:1 Scheduling
Meet weekly — consistently. Monday afternoon or Tuesday morning.
Monday afternoon or Tuesday morning is the ideal timing — it aligns with the DemandBase meeting for the ENT team and lets you set the plan for the week early. If there is nothing to talk about, that itself is something to talk about. No pipeline, no meetings, no strategy = a problem worth diagnosing together.

Qualification Standards

SQL & SQO Criteria

Know the difference cold. These criteria determine whether you get credit — and whether the opp has real legs.

⚠️
Critical Rule
BDRs must CREATE the opportunity to receive credit
Do not create an opportunity on an account you have been tag-teaming with your AE. If the AE gets them on the hook first and you want credit: you need 5 touch points with at least 2–3 different contacts in the last 30 days before you can be added to the opp.
SQL — Sales Qualified Lead

The minimum bar to move a lead into a qualified opportunity. You must gather all of these before creating the opp.

  • How many background checks do they run per year?
  • Who is their current BGC provider?
  • How long are they in their current contract? (1 year max to be able to book)
  • What time of year do they evaluate HR Tech and how often?
  • Do they use an ATS, and if so which one?
  • Confirmed persona / ICP fit
  • All ENT SQLs must be assigned accounts
  • Understanding of pain — why are they willing to take a call? What do they want to learn?

SQL notes must live on the Account Level AND in the Qualification Notes section on the opportunity.

SQO — Sales Qualified Opportunity (PINT)

The PINT framework — all four elements should be confirmed before moving to SQO.

  • P — Pain: Confidence that we can solve their specific issue
  • I — Influence: Person has influence over the decision
  • N — Next Steps: Solidified next steps with a purpose — a technical call or demo, not a "touch base"
  • T — Timeline: Realistic timeline for evaluation and decision

If next steps are scheduled without solidified pain, the opp will still progress to SQO — but pain must be confirmed before advancing further.

For RFP processes: wait until you have the RFP in hand and have confirmed you are participating before moving to qualified.

Once the meeting takes place: progression to qualified, closed lost, or a nurture plan must be identified and executed within 24 hours.

After the Meeting

Post-Meeting Expectations

What happens after a BDR-sourced meeting — and who is responsible for what.

ScenarioWho Owns ItWhat Happens
Meeting goes well → opp advances AE owns the opp from this point BDR is not responsible for follow-up post-SQO. The AE drives it to close.
Opp goes Closed Lost Account returns to BDR for nurture BDR can add to a nurture sequence or trade for a new account during quarterly refreshes.
New opp opened within 60 days of Closed Lost BDR who sourced the original opp The BDR that sourced the original opp gets credit for the new opportunity. 60-day window.
Quarterly account refreshes BDR + Manager Accounts that went CL and have been nurtured can be traded for new assigned accounts at the quarterly refresh.
How We Operate

Operating Cadence

The recurring rhythms of the BDR team. Show up to these consistently.

👤
Weekly · Mon PM or Tue AM
1:1 with Your AE Partner
Review pipeline, assign accounts for the week, talk through handoffs, and give/receive feedback on SQL quality. Come prepared — see the 1:1 Structure section above.
📞
Weekly · Tuesday & Wednesday
Outbound Call Blitz
Dedicated 1-hour team blitz. Make 20+ dials during each session. This is protected prospecting time — block it and protect it.
📊
Weekly
1:1 with Your Manager
Review KPIs, pipeline progress, activity numbers, blockers, and coaching. Come with your Athean dashboard open and your top accounts ready to discuss.
👥
Weekly
Team Meeting
Recognize wins, review pacing, peer best practices, quick trainings. Required attendance.
📰
Weekly
Sales Homeroom
Video, blog, and quiz covering product, messaging, and process updates. Required — complete on time and retain the information.
🏢
Monthly
Checkr All Hands
Company-wide goal review, wins, rotating speakers. Required attendance.
🔄
Quarterly
Account Refresh
Review and refresh your assigned account list with your manager and AE partner. Swap out closed-lost accounts that have been nurtured for fresh ICP-fit targets.
