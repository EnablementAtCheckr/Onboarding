Company overview, mission, core values, and org structure.

Sales Org Structure

The Sales team at Checkr is responsible for closing business and generating new revenue. Our sales team is broken up into specific segments:

  • Enterprise AEs — work companies with >15k employees
  • Upper Mid Market AEs — work companies with ≥5k and <15k employees
  • Commercial AEs — work companies running between 300 checks per year and <1k employees
  • SMB — companies running under 300 checks per year, sign up through our Self-Service tool

Marketing & Lead Routing

  • SDRs — follow up on anything unowned or anything coming in from a CMRL AE’s book of business
  • BDRs — follow up on any MQLs that come in on their or their AE’s (ENT) book of business
  • AEs — do not get routed MQLs

What it Means to be a BDR at Checkr

Your role, how you’re measured, and what success looks like.

Being a BDR at Checkr means that you are often the first person potential customers will ever speak with at the company. Your primary goal is to drive Sales Qualified Leads (SQLs) and Sales Qualified Opportunities (SQOs) by generating interest in Checkr’s offerings and setting meetings with contacts from within your own book of business.

Inbound (SDR)

  • Follows up on leads associated with CMRL AE or Unowned Accounts generated by marketing through events, content downloads, etc. (warm engagement leads)
  • Follows up on leads generated when prospects approach Checkr and specifically request to speak with someone on our sales team (inbound leads)
  • If you see a duplicate MQL routing to an SDR, reach out to the SDR with the link to your correct account and they will reassign it to you

Outbound (BDR — your role!)

  • Performs cold outreach into a set of target accounts
  • Sources contacts to reach out to within a given account through tools such as LinkedIn Sales Navigator and ZoomInfo
  • Works closely with AEs to penetrate target account lists and create new business opportunities
  • Follows up on MQLs associated with your book of business

How BDRs are Measured

BDRs are measured on the number of SQOs (Sales Qualified Opportunities) that are created and confirmed by the AE team.

  • CMRL BDR: 7 SQOs per month / 21 SQOs per quarter
  • ENT BDR: 4 SQOs per month / 12 SQOs per quarter

Results are the outcome of the right behaviors and activity. Below are the key KPIs for a fully ramped BDR — first two month expectations will be provided separately during onboarding.

Performance Evaluation Framework

  • We have a quarterly as well as annual process for goal setting and performance evaluations
  • Each quarter you will self-reflect on how you stacked up to your previous goals, and set new goals for the new quarter
  • At the end of the year, all quarters are combined for an annual performance review
  • Your rating can impact things like sabbatical approval and promotion eligibility

Performance Management

  • As an Outbound BDR you are expected to maintain consistent performance against SQO and pipeline targets
  • If consistent performance is not achieved, you may be placed on a coaching memo or subsequently moved to a performance improvement plan (PIP)
  • The goal of these plans is to find the right structure and process for success so that it can be replicated each month

Positioning Checkr

Value propositions, Command of the Message, ICP, competitors, and account prioritization.

Checkr’s Value Propositions

A value proposition is a statement made by a company that summarizes why a customer should use their product or service. When you are asked what Checkr does, a good way to answer this question is to use a value proposition.

  • Value propositions should not be about Checkr’s solutions or features — they are about our prospects, the pains we solve, and the types of customers we help
  • Value propositions will change depending on what type of prospect you are speaking to

Command of the Message (CoM)

CoM is the framework we use for the full sales cycle at Checkr, composed of 6 main components:

  1. The Before Scenario, and the Negative Consequences of it (Prime for BDRs to uncover!)
  2. The After Scenario, and the Positive Business Outcomes from it
  3. The Required Capabilities, and the Metrics to prove success
  4. How Checkr Does It, and how we do it Better (Our differentiators)
  5. Proof Points, such as customer references and case studies
  6. Discovery / Trapping Questions to determine what’s driving decision-making

Sequences: Emails, Calls & LinkedIn

Outreach sequences, email templates, call scripts, objection handling, and LinkedIn strategy.

What is a Sequence?

A prospecting sequence is a detailed step-by-step plan of activities designed to maximize the likelihood of making contact with a prospect in order to convert them into an SQL.

  • Length — total number of days from initial outreach to the last step. If no contact is made throughout the entire sequence, move the contact to a passive nurture sequence
  • Steps — individual actions taken, corresponding to a channel (email, phone call, LinkedIn message)

Checkr Sequences

BDRs at Checkr work out of a collection of sequences titled “BDR Outbound”. This collection follows a categorization strategy of “Above The Line” vs “Below The Line” sequences for our ICPs, as well as sequences specific to ATS integrations, personas, events and more.

  • Below The Line (BTL) — For Individual Contributors and Front-line managers. Less personalization, more automation, fewer total steps
  • Above The Line (ATL) — For Directors, VPs, and above (c-suite). Higher level of manual personalization, more steps, greater attention toward key stakeholders

Emails

  • Templates — pre-built messaging from marketing and enablement. It is an expectation and requirement that you do proper research and add personalization to emails prior to sending. Automated and un-personalized emails should only be used for low tier prospects, later stage emails in the sequence, or if you are truly unable to find a compelling trigger
  • Snippets — act like an email template shortcut, allowing you to drop smaller bits of text, links, or paragraphs into emails at the click of a button

Calls — Best Practices

  • Take a no stone left unturned approach when identifying the right numbers to call
  • Don’t be afraid to select options like “sales” or “customer support” to connect with a live person who can direct you
  • If a call goes directly to voicemail always call right back — a double dial can circumvent spam blockers
  • Mark phone numbers accordingly in Outreach (valid, questionable, invalid)
  • Keep detailed notes in your call notes for each call on anything relevant for the future

Qualification Questions

  • What is your role at (company name) and in what ways are you involved with your company’s background checks process?
  • How many background checks does your company perform annually? Do you anticipate this number growing over the next year?
  • Who are you currently using? Do you have an exclusive contract with them — if so when does it end?
  • What types of screening does your company require for background checks?
  • Does your company use an ATS (applicant tracking system) today? If yes, which system?
  • Optional: Do you have a timeline in which you hope to make a decision on your next background check provider?

Probing Questions

  • What does your current background check process look like?
  • What’s one thing your company does really well with hiring? What’s one thing you’re trying to improve?
  • When was the last time (company name) reevaluated their background check process?
  • Do you have a sense of (company name)’s typical turnaround time for background checks?
  • Are you aware of any initiatives at (company name) around fair hiring practices?

Objection Handling

  • AAA Method: Acknowledge, Address, Ask — acknowledge their objection with a neutral statement, address it by pulling in Checkr value, then re-engage with an open-ended question
  • The “Exactly” Method — “That’s exactly why I called you!” Take the wind out of their objection by flipping it around and connecting it to the reason you were reaching out

LinkedIn

  • Always attempt to connect first — keep your connect message vague if you send one at all
  • If the person connects with you, message them as soon as possible, especially if you can see they are online
  • If after a few days the prospect has not connected, use an InMail credit (you get 50 a month — max these out)
  • LinkedIn messaging is typically less pushy on the CTA and more informative, looking for validation on the right person
  • Leverage content from our ICP playbooks to send via LinkedIn
  • AEs should own ATL for Tier 1 — BDRs divide and conquer on BTL
  • KPI: 1 LinkedIn video message a day / 5 per week

Working Leads

High-level process, MQL workflow, disqualifying, recycling, and lead hygiene.

High-Level Process Overview

Start with Research & Call Quickly

  1. Open the lead in SFDC
  2. Research for 3 minutes — review campaign history, lead’s title and company info, identify triggers and elements for personalization
  3. Call/email the prospect using Outreach — 1-hour maximum SLA on actioning leads, so ensure your email notifications are on
  4. Log and complete the call

The Lead Answers — What Happens Next?

  1. Qualify the Lead based on our SQL Criteria — schedule the next call at the earliest availability on the aligned AE’s calendar
  2. The Lead does not qualify:
    • Not a good fit right now or is unresponsive → Recycle
    • Will never be a good fit for Checkr → Disqualify

Disqualifying

We only want to DQ leads that aren’t going to use Checkr now or ever. BDRs will manually update this in SFDC.

  • Candidate — mark as “candidate”
  • Spam — mark as “Bad Data”
  • No longer at company
  • Competitor — they are a competitor of ours
  • No Permissible Purpose

Recycling

Sometimes the lead or contact company is a good target, but they are not ready to buy just yet. BDRs should mark these as Recycled. When leads go all the way through a prospecting sequence without responding, move them into Recycle to end our outreach and allow marketing to re-target the prospect.

Lead Hygiene

Lead hygiene refers to our ability to follow processes and keep up with all of our scheduled outreach activities — keeping up with daily metrics and updating any necessary fields.

BDR Dashboard

The BDR Dashboard is your starting point when opening Salesforce. Within it you can see any MQLs needing attention from not being worked, not being called when part of an automated sequence, or that have gone stale. The BDR dashboard is also home to key KPIs including booked meetings, pipeline, and activities.

Managing Duplicates

  • If you see a Lead and Contact dupe: From the lead → Convert → “Choose Existing” → uncheck “Don’t create an opportunity upon conversion”
  • Send Merge Request
  • If you don’t own the account or get an error: Slack Andi directly with a link to the record and a request to merge

Warm Engagements

Warm Engagements are people that have interacted with Checkr in some way — webinar attendees, content downloads, event registrants, or associates with a campaign. Unlike an MQL, a warm engagement is not a hand raise. They have not explicitly asked to be contacted, but given their engagement with us they are certainly warm enough to have a conversation.

Marketo Sales Insights (MSI)

MSI is a web-based app that connects Marketo information directly onto Leads or Contacts in SFDC. With MSI you’ll have a holistic view into the actions your prospects are taking — pages they’re visiting, emails they’re reading, and campaigns they’re engaging with.

  • Insights tab — aggregate timeline of activity by interesting moment and email outreach
  • Interesting Moments — historical list of actions defined by Marketo, categorized as Email, Web, and Milestones
  • Stars — “Relative Score” compared to the entire database (1 Star = average, 3 Stars = high)
  • Flames — “Relative Urgency,” meaning how frequently and by how much the score has changed recently

Handoff & Salesforce Conversion

How to qualify, coordinate, and complete the opportunity handoff to AEs.

Opportunity Handoff

After creating an opportunity there are two key actions a BDR needs to take:

  1. Coordinate the handoff of the opportunity with the appropriate Account Executive (your aligned AE for ENT or leveraging the CMRL AE Round Robin for CMRL)
  2. Properly convert any lead that was directly related to the opp creation in Salesforce, or work off of the contact that the meeting was booked with
  3. Create a new opportunity and add all notes — hold off on assigning ownership to the AE until after we know it is moving to qualified

Scheduled Meetings

  • Prioritize the prospect’s earliest available time that is also available for their aligned AE
  • Only schedule calls between 8am–5pm local time on the AE’s calendar unless explicitly stated otherwise
  • Book the meeting directly on the AE’s calendar

Adding Qualification Notes

The BDR will add qualifying notes to the opportunity notes field in the Stage 0 opportunity and associate one primary contact on the opp record. Fill in all fields of relevant information — ATS, volume of checks, why change why now, types of checks, prospect’s role in eval, etc. Notes should be in the corresponding fields, not all in one paragraph.

Facilitating the Handoff / Discovery Call

  1. BDR sends an email intro to AE and Customer
  2. BDR attends the intro meeting with the AE in order to:
    • Briefly recap the previous call
    • Reiterate any goals for the second call
    • Introduce the AE as the next point of contact and hand off the call
    • Important: This intro and handoff from the BDR should take under one minute
    • BDR and AE should be on time, if not a few minutes early
    • BDR is expected to remain on the call for the duration to help take notes and shadow the AE

Post Call-Actions

  • If Qualified: AE updates sales stage as appropriate. Congrats — you’ve just earned yourself an SQO!
  • If NOT Qualified:
    • If waiting on a few pieces for qualification: opp stays in BDR’s name and BDR works with AE until criteria is met
    • If not going to be qualified: BDR updates the sales stage to “Closed-Lost” with the appropriate reason
  • If NO SHOW: Opp stays in BDR’s name — BDR is responsible for follow-up/rescheduling. Update next step fields on the opportunity and make every effort to reschedule within 1 month. If unsuccessful, BDR close-loses the Stage 0 opp

Creating an Opportunity in Salesforce from a Contact

Navigate to the contact record of the prospect you booked your meeting with, find the “Create Opportunity” button on the top right corner. Select “Sales Opportunity” for BDR generated opps (new business). Ensure the opportunity is linked to the correct account, is in the correct forecast category (“interested”), and has a close date set for 4 months out.

Salesforce Conversion (from a Lead)

If the account already exists:

  1. Click “Convert” from within the lead
  2. Select “Existing Account” and select the appropriate account
  3. Uncheck “Don’t create an opportunity upon conversion”
  4. Follow the same process as converting from contacts when filling out opportunity details

If there is no existing account:

  1. Click “Convert” from within the lead
  2. Select “New Account” and input the appropriate account name
  3. Select “New Contact” to convert your lead into a contact
  4. Uncheck “Don’t create an opportunity upon conversion”
  5. Convert the lead into a contact
